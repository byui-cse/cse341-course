Course Specific Setup for Instructor

Microsoft Teams

Create a Microsoft Teams team for your course. This is where the majority of communication will take place instead of I-learn discussion boards or email. You will use your BYU-Idaho email to create the team for your course, then it will be easy to invite your students to your team because they will all have Microsoft accounts with the university. Here are some instructions on how to create a team in Microsoft Teams if you have not done it before.

Online courses are monitored by Teaching Group Leads (TGLs) to ensure that students are having a great experience. One of the items these leads look to see is that instructors are having good discussion with the students. In most courses this occurs in I-Learn discussion boards, so the TGL may not be aware of the Microsoft Teams discussions. To help keep them in the loop, after announcing Microsoft Teams to the students and adding them to the team via their byui email address, please also invite the TGL to join.

Welcome Email

Send a welcome email to your students to establish expectations and initiate your desired learning environment. Please ensure your email contains the following information:

  • A welcome to the class, including information about when the class will start and an explanation of how to access the course.

  • An invitation to reserve 3–4 hours each lesson per credit to devote to their studies. Time should be reserved throughout the lesson to successfully meet course deadlines.

  • A brief explanation of any unique course characteristics.

  • An invitation to access the course promptly and an explanation of what students should do to get started.

  • Remind students that this is not an independent study course, so they need to start completing assignments right away.

  • Provide your contact information, including your BYU-Idaho email address (and a phone number, if you want to provide one).

  • Remind students to use appropriate etiquette and respect in their interactions with instructors and students.

  • Holding an office hour is optional. If you intend to hold an office hour, invite students to attend, and provide instruction on how to find your virtual office.
